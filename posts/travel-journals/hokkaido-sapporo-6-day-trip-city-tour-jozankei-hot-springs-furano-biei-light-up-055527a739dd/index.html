[Travelogue] 2026 Hokkaido Sapporo 6-Day Trip — Sapporo City, Jozankei Onsen, Hokkaido Light-Up (Furano Fairy Platform & Biei Blue Pond) One-Day Tour

A Complete Record of Exploring Sapporo City, Soaking in Jozankei Onsen, and Visiting Furano Fairy Hill and Biei Blue Pond Light-Up Tour

I have been working at my new job for almost half a year and just finished a project. Since I had never been to snowy Japan before, I decided to take a trip to Hokkaido in mid-March.

Preparation Before Departure

Plan

  • Day 1: After arriving in Sapporo, go straight to Jozankei Onsen for an overnight stay

  • Day 2: Return to Sapporo City to visit attractions

  • Day 3: Otaru, Otaru Aquarium

  • Day 4: KKday Hokkaido Illumination Tour ｜ Furano Fairy Hill & Biei Blue Pond & Shirahige Waterfall One-Day Tour｜Departing from Sapporo

  • Day 5: Shopping and Hokkaido Shrine

  • Day 6: Head to the airport for departure

Line

Flight — China Airlines

  • 🛫 03/15 Departure CI130: TPE Taoyuan International Airport T2 08:35 (actual delay 3 minutes) -> CTS New Chitose Airport, Hokkaido 13:15 (actual delay 7 minutes)

  • 🛬 03/20 Return flight CI131: CTS New Chitose Airport 14:20 (actual delay 8 minutes) -> TPE Taoyuan International Airport 18:15 (actual arrival 3 minutes early)

Price: NT $24,356 per person

*Because the snow season is still ongoing, prices remain high. Returning on Friday is cheaper as weekends cost even more…

Sapporo Station <-> Jozankei Round-Trip Bus Ticket

https://jozankei.jp/tw/news/newaccess/

There are many JR trains from New Chitose Airport to Sapporo Station, and since the flight arrival time is uncertain, I didn’t buy tickets in advance. To get to Jozankei Onsen, you can only take a bus. According to the latest transportation information as of December 2025, there are three routes to choose from:

  1. [Reservation Required] [About 60 minutes] Sapporo Station → Take Kappa Liner Bus → Jozankei Onsen.
    Click here to view the timetable

  2. [No reservation needed] [About 70 minutes] Sapporo Station → Makomanai Station → Bus → Jozankei Onsen.

  3. [No reservation needed] [About 120 minutes] New Chitose Airport → Airport Bus (Hokuto Kotsu) → Makomanai Station → Jozankei Onsen.

Route (1) — かっぱライナー号 / Kappa Liner is the smoothest and most convenient. I checked the schedule, and after the flight lands, going through immigration, then taking the JR to Sapporo Station and walking out, the 4:00 PM departure (limited to autumn and winter) has plenty of time. So I booked online in advance (foreigners can also book!).

Outbound Trip Sapporo Station -> Jozankei:

https://www.jotetsu.co.jp/bus/kappa_liner/index.html

  • Note that the schedules in red operate only in autumn and winter. The buses departing from Sapporo Station at 16:00 and 17:00 only go as far as the Jozankei Garage.

Return trip Jozankei -> Sapporo Station:

https://www.jotetsu.co.jp/bus/kappa_liner/index.html

  • For the return trip, I planned to check out at 11:00 and stroll along the hot spring street before leaving, so I booked the 13:15 departure bus (boarding at Shirahige Falls at 13:31).

Reservation Method:

  1. Register as a member on the official reservation website

  • Email / Taiwan mobile number OK.

  • Be sure to remember your account and password, as the e-ticket is activated online directly for the driver to verify.

  1. Choose to purchase the かっぱライナー号 / Kappa Liner round-trip or one-way ticket

  1. Number of Participants

  1. Select the departure date and flight (flight times are shown as departure from Sapporo)

  1. Choose the pick-up and drop-off locations for the outbound trip

Boarding and alighting stations can be referred to in the chart below:

https://www.jotetsu.co.jp/bus/kappa_liner/index.html

Our hotel is closer to (2) Shiraito Falls/Shiraitonotaki, so we booked to get off at this stop.

Later, I found out you can continue riding the loop and get off at (7) Jozankei Onsen Higashi 2 Chome / 定山渓温泉東2丁目 stop, which is right at the hotel entrance.

  1. Select the return date and schedule (schedule times are based on the departure station), boarding and alighting locations

  2. Complete payment with online credit card transaction

Price: ¥2,920 JPY/adult.

You will receive an email confirmation after the reservation is successful. No need to exchange tickets in person; just activate the ticket online on the ticket website and show it to the driver.

Day 1 (03/15) — Arrival in Sapporo, Hokkaido and Jozankei Onsen Hotel

05:00 Airport Transfer Departure

05:35 Airport Transfer Arrive at TPE Taoyuan International Airport Terminal 2 (T2)

Assigned to the corners, the counter opens only at 06:10.

Activate the Japan eSIM during your free time.

06:40 Completed check-in, baggage drop-off, and security screening

Check-in and baggage drop likely took little time because we arrived early. There were quite a few people at immigration, but the process moved quickly. We cleared immigration around 06:40.

Leisurely having breakfast Zzzzz.

07:30 Waiting and strolling — D6 Boarding Gate

There is still some time before boarding, so first go to D1 Sit on a Free Massage Chair (tokens can be obtained from nearby shops).

07:50 Prepare for boarding

08:38 Plane takes off TPE -> CTS

The plane was a newer China Airlines Airbus A330–300, with spacious seating and well-equipped in-flight entertainment on the screens.

The food was just… so-so.

Flight time: about 3 hours and 50 minutes, snacks will also be provided.

13:22 Plane lands TPE -> CTS

14:10 Completed immigration and baggage claim

Immigration was quite fast, but I waited a long time for my luggage. Not sure if it was because there were too many people (all seats were sold out).

Follow the signs for “Domestic Terminal, JR Line” all the way from the airport.

After reaching the other building’s lobby, go down to B1F to find the JR station. (If using the escalator, you need to take a bit of a detour.)

Timetable

14:29 Take the Rapid Service from New Chitose Airport Station to Sapporo Station

https://www.jrhokkaido.co.jp/global/chinese/travel/airport.html

  • Sapporo <-> New Chitose Airport takes about 33 - 41 minutes; IC cards like Kitaca, Suica, PASMO can be used, no need to queue at ticket machines, with 163 trips per day, very frequent.

  • 5 sections unreserved seats, 1 section reserved seat

You can enter the station directly with a Suica card, but I wanted to get change, so I still bought a ticket with coins.

15:06 Arrive at Sapporo Station

  • Used the restroom and bought some food, then continued walking to bus stop 27.

15:15 Head to Sapporo Station Bus Stop No. 27 (Kappa Line Boarding Point)

15:25 Arrive at Sapporo Station Bus Stop No. 27 (Kappa Line Boarding Point) Waiting for the bus

Exit Sapporo Station Front, turn left, then right at the next intersection, and you will see it. There is a red building behind.

  • Japanese service is reliable; staff here will also confirm that you are going to Jozankei and have a reservation.

16:00 Departure to Jozankei Onsen

  • Show the activation screen on your phone login reservation website to the staff, and you can board directly.

  • Since it is a sightseeing bus, you can hand your suitcase directly to the staff to store it in the luggage compartment.

  • The drive takes about 50 minutes. By mid-March, there is little snow in the city, but some snow remains in the suburbs.

16:50 Arrive at Jozankei Onsen (2) Shiraito Falls / 白糸の滝 stop and get off

  • The Kappa Line bus design features the mascot of Jozankei Onsen — the kappa.

  • Show the electronic ticket to the driver when getting off and inform them that you need to collect your luggage

  • Get off the bus and walk straight ahead across the street to reach the hot spring hotel where we will stay tonight!

17:00 Arrive at Jozankei Yurakusoan ゆらく草庵 Japanese Onsen Hotel

Very traditional Japanese style, the entire hotel is made of wood. Upon entering the lobby, there is tatami flooring, and you need to change shoes and put them in the shoe cabinet.

  • 房間很大有獨立的廁所、私人衛浴/湯屋、客廳跟床；厲害的是雖然是傳統木造榻榻米但是整體維護的非常好， 沒有任何髒髒舊舊或是霉味的感覺 。
  • 也有公共的私人湯屋可以使用 (有好幾間，沒人就可以進去)

18:00 Head Out for Dinner

The hotel offers a plan with one night and two meals, but we booked one night with breakfast only; we went out to find food.

18:10 Kita no Kuni｜Jozankei Manseikaku Hotel Milione 3rd Floor

The hot spring town mostly offers accommodation with meals, so there are few restaurants outside; we finally walked to the izakaya on the 3rd floor of another hotel, 万世閣, for dinner.

  • First Drink: Draft — Sapporo Draft Beer

  • Ate pork cutlet rice and the local specialty fried crucian carp (quite delicious).

19:30 Return to the hotel for hot spring bath and rest

  • Fortunately, there were many convenience stores along the way, so I stopped by FamilyMart to buy a late-night snack for the evening.

Although it stopped snowing, it still gets very cold after sunset. After a long day, I returned to the hotel early to soak in the hot spring and rest.

Day 2 (03/16 Mon) — Jozankei Onsen Street, Sapporo City Attractions

09:00 Have breakfast

I happened to get a window seat, and the view outside was very beautiful.

The breakfast was very hearty: mainly rice/vegetable rice, miso soup, (1) steamed pork with vegetables in the middle, (2) cold salmon — can be heated using the alcohol lamp and aluminum foil in the center, (3) tamagoyaki.

11:00 Checkout, Store Luggage, Walk Around Jozankei Onsen Area

There was still some time before boarding the return bus at 13:31, so I took a walk around the entire Jozankei Onsen area.

11:10 Jozankei Shrine

The entire shrine was frozen under heavy snow.

11:30 Kappa Family’s Wishing Hand Bath

Wash your hands briefly.

The slope beside leads down to the hot spring park.

11:40 Jozankei Onsen Park

The park has a public foot bath and a hot spring outlet where you can boil water and eggs.

If you want to try cooking onsen eggs, you can cross the bridge to the opposite side and buy onsen eggs at ㈱Jozankei Bussankan Central Store, then cook them and bring them back to the shop to eat.

12:00 Passing by ふる川果日 J.glacée

Continuing uphill, I passed a bakery with freshly baked apple croissants. I bought one to try, and it tasted good.

12:15 Passing by Iwato Kannon Hall

After passing here, continue forward and then down to return to the starting hotel.

You can imagine how heavy the snow was before; the suspension bridge over the creek was completely frozen with snow and ice!

12:30 Walk back to the hotel

With some time left, we continued to visit the nearby Shiraito Falls.

We had to take a detour downhill; unfortunately, it was closed due to being frozen by snow and ice.

13:10 Return to the hotel to get luggage

13:15 Arrive at (2) Shirahige Falls Bus Stop (opposite side from arrival)

Our bus departs from the starting station at 13:15 and is expected to arrive at Shirahige Falls station at 13:31. The waiting time is quite boring, so we stepped on the snow to pass the time.

13:40 Board the bus back to Sapporo Station

  • Show the e-ticket to the driver once when boarding and getting off.

14:50 Return to Sapporo, check in at Keio Prelia Hotel Sapporo : 京王プレリアホテル札幌 : 札幌京王普雷利亞飯店

  • 飯店很新、設備齊全，房間算大。
  • 地點在後站比較不熱鬧，要走一小段、札幌站也沒藥妝店； 相較之下大通站周邊比較熱鬧。

Around 15:20, we packed up and set off again. We hadn’t had lunch, planning to have dinner near Odori Park early.

15:30 Yodobashi

On the way from the hotel to the JR/subway station, there is a Yodobashi (with a McDonald’s behind it). I checked out the capsule toys there.

16:10 Arrive at Susukino Station

Take the train to Susukino Station to get ready for Sapporo’s famous “lamb” barbecue.

16:15 Genghis Khan Daruma Main Store Start Eating

  • Ordered two signature dishes, one regular meat dish, white rice, and draft beer (large size… a bit too large).

  • The meat was very delicious, with no gamey taste at all, and the texture was very tender.

  • Eating this much costs about 700 TWD per person.

The restaurant is small, and there are branches nearby. By the time we finished eating around 5:00 PM, many people were already lining up outside.

17:00 Susukino Station Intersection

After eating, slowly walk back to Odori Station. I wanted to visit the Sapporo TV Tower, passing by Sapporo’s most famous crossroads.

17:15 Stop by Tanukikoji for Gachapon and Don Quijote

There is a whole building MEGA Don Quijote here.

17:41 Sapporo TV Tower

18:30 Sapporo TV Tower Night View

Stayed at Sapporo Tower until about 18:30 and came down after enjoying the night view.

You can also collect stamps while gathering postcards.

18:40 PARCO Sapporo Shopping

Right outside the TV Tower, the next intersection is Parco Sapporo. Let’s go shopping.

20:30 Buy convenience store late-night snacks and return to the hotel to rest

Dinner was eaten early, so I had to eat more for the late-night snack; I bought convenience store instant noodles, skewers, fried chicken, strawberry fruit wine (delicious), and Whiskey Chocolate Ice Cream in collaboration with Hokkaido The Sapporo Bar Monogatari (it really tastes alcoholic and is quite good).

Day 3 (03/17 Tue) — Otaru, Otaru Aquarium

09:30 Leave the house

Bought a McDonald’s breakfast to eat in the car.

10:00 Board at Sapporo Station to Otaru Station

The result was a local train, very crowded with no seats, making it impossible to eat on the train.

10:42 Arrive at Otaru Station

Just by looking at the crowd, you can tell many people have come to Otaru.

10:45 Otaru Station

Exit Otaru Station from the front, cross the street, and the bus stop is on the right. Wait directly at platform 3 for the 10 Otaru Aquarium Line:

Image source: Google Map - K T (torute)

  • 10 Otaru Aquarium Line: It is almost direct and takes about 20 minutes to reach Otaru Aquarium.

  • 11 Shukutsu Line: Not direct, it also goes there but takes about 30 minutes.

11:10 Arrive at Otaru Aquarium

Simply show the online purchased 【Official Sales】Hokkaido Otaru Aquarium Ticket (Instant Use/Valid Within 30 Days of Purchase) QR Code at the counter to enter directly.

Otaru Aquarium Homemade Map

  • The Ferris wheel is closed during winter and is not inside the aquarium area, so a separate ticket is required.

  • Although Otaru Aquarium is not large, it has a rich variety of species and is well maintained; there are many shows, making the overall experience quite good.

  • The red line marks the penguin walking route

Opening hours vary by season. Please refer to the official website.

Turn right after entering and go down — Sea Animal Park

There is only an escalator going uphill here; the downhill is quite shaky.

All outdoor performances are here:

  • Seal Activity: The show was happening at 11:20 when we arrived

  • Sea Lion Show (Giant Sea Lion): 11:40

  • Penguin Picnic: 12:00

  • Penguin activity: Not seen

  • Walrus Feeding Explanation: Not seen

11:40 Hokkaido Lion Show

Arrived early and stood in the front row; the northern sea lion is really huge!

Seal

After watching the show, next door is the seal pool, where each seal looks very relaxed.

You can buy fish to feed the seals and sea lions for interaction, but it’s limited and some pools do not allow feeding, so please check the signs.

12:00 Penguin Walk

The complete route is shown by the red line in the above image, ending at the penguin pool for a bubble bath.

After watching the seals, head to the penguin walking route. There is a white line on the floor; stand outside the line and wait for the penguins to pass by. I stood at the corner here (the position of the person in the photo above).

It’s a little penguin, somewhat like the Nankan penguin.

Eventually, we will reach the penguin pool to soak in the water:

Also check out other indoor animals: (seals, walruses)
