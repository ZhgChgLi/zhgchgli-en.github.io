[Guide] 2026 Atami Summer Fireworks Festival Complete Guide: Viewing Spots, Transportation, Food, Accommodation, Nearby Attractions, One-Day Itinerary

The most charming aspect of the Atami fireworks is that it is “very close to Tokyo” yet offers a “seaside festival vibe”:
After getting off the Shinkansen and walking a short distance, you reach the beach. Lay out a picnic mat on the sand and wait for the show. The fireworks bloom over the sea and night sky simultaneously. After the show, you can enjoy hot springs and seafood—this is the romance of Atami.

If you just want to catch the key points first, read this section:

  • Main Venue: Atami Bay (Recommended free viewing spot: Atami Sun Beach)

  • Typical Start Time: In July and August, usually 20:15–20:40; in other months, typically 20:20–20:40 (subject to official announcements)

  • From Tokyo to Atami: About 45 minutes by Shinkansen; about a 15–25 minute walk from JR Atami Station to the seaside (longer during crowds)

  • Biggest Pitfall: It’s not “finding a spot,” but crowds leaving for the station + queuing for tickets
    → Solution: Buy return tickets in advance, allow buffer time, bring a picnic mat to secure your spot early

1. What Makes the Atami Fireworks Festival So Special? (Why It’s Worth a Trip)

The Atami Sea Fireworks Festival has been held since 1952 and is one of Atami’s most iconic events. Surrounded by mountains on three sides, Atami Bay’s bowl-shaped terrain causes the fireworks sounds to echo off the mountain walls, often described as having the acoustics of a large open-air stadium. Combined with the reflections on the water and the grand finale “Great Aerial Niagara,” the live experience is very impressive.

2. How to Choose a Viewing Spot? The Best Free Viewing Areas and Routes

1) First Choice: Atami Sun Beach

This is the most popular and intuitive choice: wide view, comfortable seating, and great angles for photos.
If it’s your first time at the Atami fireworks, choosing here is basically a safe bet.

If you want to “sit comfortably on the beach with an unobstructed view,” my suggestion is:
Arrive between 17:30 and 18:30 for the best timing (arrive earlier on busy weekends or holidays; if you arrive late, you can usually still find a spot, but it may be further back).

2) Plan Your Exit: Move Back Towards the Tokai Coast Area

The center of the beach is the liveliest spot but gets more crowded when leaving; if you want to get back to the station faster after the event, it’s better to choose a spot further back (near Tokai Coast Town or the later part of the promenade). The view is still great, and exiting is smoother.

3) Stock up before entering: Shopping street / convenience store → beach

At Atami, it’s ideal to “buy delicious food and drinks at the station front or shopping street” first, then picnic by the beach while waiting for the fireworks.
You can also reserve your spot with a picnic mat, then take a walk nearby or buy drinks.

3. Transportation Guide: How to Get There with Ease? (From Tokyo / From Airport / Return After the Event)

1) Departure from Tokyo: The Shinkansen is the Most Reliable Choice

Atami is located along the Tokaido Line and Tokaido Shinkansen, making it very convenient to take the Shinkansen from Tokyo/Shinagawa to Atami.
If you are planning a “same-day round trip,” it is recommended to do at least these two things:

  1. Buy return tickets in advance (or confirm e-ticket/QR Code entry method)

  2. Allow 20–30 minutes to return to the station (uphill exit + crowds)

You can also use KKday’s JR section to check ticket types and compare prices

Walking from JR Atami Station to the Beach

After exiting the station, walk through the shopping street in front of the station and head downhill toward the beach to reach the sand. You can follow this pace:

  1. Prepare in advance: Buy drinks, food, and picnic mats (or at least plastic sheets) at the shopping street or convenience stores

  2. Reserve a Spot on the Beach: Lay your mat first, then take a walk around or use the restroom.

2) Departure from Airport: For a Hassle-Free Experience, Choose Private Transfer or Shuttle Service

If you are traveling with elderly family members, carrying a lot of luggage, or heading straight to Atami upon arrival (without transfers), private transfer will be much more convenient.

3) Leaving and Returning: The Most Important Thing Is Not Walking, but “Entering the Station”

The official notice also emphasizes: After the fireworks end, 21:00–22:00, Atami Station will be very crowded. It is recommended to buy return tickets and top up your IC card in advance, and adjust your departure time earlier or later to avoid peak hours.
If you are heading towards Ito/Shimoda, sometimes you can consider taking the train from “JR Kinomiya Station” to help disperse the crowd (follow official guidance and the situation on the day).

Useful Tips (Must-See for Same-Day Round Trip)

  • Around 18:00, there is usually still space on the beach (but arrive earlier during peak season/weekends).

5. How to Eat on Fireworks Day? Must-Try Foods in Atami and the “Most Practical” Dining Strategy

Atami is a hot spring tourist spot with a wide variety of food options, but on fireworks days, you need to think in terms of “efficiency.”

The Most Practical Strategy: Buy First at Convenience Stores/Shopping Streets, Picnic on the Beach for Comfort

The reason is simple:
Popular restaurants will be full and have long lines on the day of the fireworks; if you schedule dinner 1–2 hours before the show starts, you will likely feel rushed.

Must-Eat List in Atami (Add More If You Have Time)

  • Seafood: Sashimi, seafood bowls, grilled fish set meals (very popular in Atami/Izu)

  • Desserts: Atami Pudding, Ice Cream

  • Souvenirs: Dried fish, hot spring steamed buns

6. How to Choose Accommodation? Booking Tips for “Watching Fireworks”

The three most common types of accommodation in Atami are:
Onsen ryokan (with breakfast and dinner) / Ocean-view hotels / Budget business hotels.

You can quickly decide using these three conditions

  1. Location: Near the station (easy to get around) vs Near the sea (convenient for watching fireworks and walking)

  2. Meals: Should you have a “ryokan dinner”? (Restaurant lines are longer on fireworks days)

  3. Room View: If you want to watch the fireworks from your room → choose a “high-floor sea-facing” room and check if it offers a view (based on the hotel’s announcement)

Recommended Booking Strategies (Very Useful on Fireworks Days)

  • Book Early: Hotel availability on fireworks days (especially in July–August) fills up quickly. The earlier you book, the better chance you have to get a “sea view” room.

  • Decide if you want to rush for the train first:

    • Want to Relax → Stay One Night in Atami (or Kinomiya)

    • From Tokyo → Treat the “return Shinkansen time” as a fixed schedule constraint

  • Avoid having dinner during the showtime: If staying with a one-night, two-meal plan, it is recommended to choose options that allow early dining or flexible scheduling.

  • Keyword Selection: Sea View (オーシャンビュー/Sea View), Hot Spring (温泉), Station Shuttle (送迎/シャトル)

Room rates and availability on fireworks days vary greatly. It is recommended to prioritize “cancellable plans” and book early. Whether the room has a fireworks view depends on the hotel’s announcement or room description.

7. Culture and History: Why is Atami Ideal for Fireworks Viewing?

Atami is not just about fireworks; it is a true “resort city.”
On one side, there is the bay and the beach, and on the other, hills and hot springs. This terrain allows you to experience all of the following in a single evening:

  • The Open Feeling of Watching Fireworks by the Sea

  • The Immersive Impact Brought by the Echo from the Mountain Walls

  • The Relaxing End of Soaking in a Hot Spring After the Event

Another important thing to know: Atami has long been famous for its hot springs and is one of the most accessible hot spring areas near the Kanto region.
If you plan this trip as “watch fireworks at night, soak in hot springs at night or the next day,” the whole Atami experience will feel very well-paced.
